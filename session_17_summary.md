## THE ARCHIVE — ALDRIN

Eden told Aldrin directly what happened with Lyren two nights ago. The loss of control. The confession. The dark temptations named out loud. Aldrin received it the way good archivists receive things — completely, without collapsing or dramatizing. Disappointed briefly in the loss of control. Not at all disappointed in what came after.

### The Name Under Everything

Aldrin revealed what two days of archival research had produced. The name that referred Aldric Voss to the Crown Council thirty-two years ago was not acting alone — and was himself placed by someone else. The chain of recommendations extends back at least fifty years through the archive. Possibly longer. The records before fifty years are incomplete. Possibly deliberately.

The chain doesn't end with a person. It ends with something that doesn't die.

Aldrin's quiet conclusion, held for twenty years: *"Something has been pursuing a specific agenda across multiple Councils, multiple Kings."* Sable had been getting close to knowing. Which is why she died.

Eden's word for it: **lich**.

Aldrin didn't know the word. Understood immediately.

**Key realization:** Voss wasn't the architect. He was a piece. The slim volume in the sub-basement was never just a breadcrumb. It was a map. To something older, darker, and considerably more patient than anything encountered in twenty-three days.

## TORMUND — FIRST VISIT

### The Disc

Tormund had been preparing off-camera. A small disc of dark material — not quite metal, not quite stone — with lines that emerged during construction. Not drawn by Tormund. Drawn by the Weave itself. The Weave's immune response to something that has been wounding it for fifty years, possibly centuries.

**The disc's properties:**
- Blocks all divination-based location attempts while on Eden's person
- Effect extends approximately 15 feet in all directions
- Requires skin contact or pocket proximity; degrades beyond arm's length
- Removable — the effect dissipates within two minutes of removal
- Works both ways: also blocks the polygon from finding Eden while active

**Critical limitation:** A moving 15-foot blind spot in a crowded area is itself a beacon to a sophisticated observer. The disc requires judgment, not habit.

**Origin:** Tormund constructed it from a theoretical framework developed from Sable's companion text — a second slim volume in the Archive, older, in Sable's handwriting, arguing the counter-position to the philosophical defense of lichdom. The companion text was Sable's research. The disc was her work finding its hands.

### The Companion Text — First Mention

Tormund confirmed the existence of Sable's counter-research. Held in the Archive. Third shelf. A different hand, a different era. Beginning mid-thought, as if Sable had been arguing this in her head for years before writing it down.

*"Some things stay in sub-basements until the right person finds them."*

## VAHLEN — THE CONSERVATORY

### Lyren Is Off-Limits

Eden led with this before any intelligence. Before the slim volume. Before the ask. The tremor present, not hidden.

*"She keeps herself. Whatever you're planning. Not a hair falls from her head unless she accepts the danger. Which I think she will. But you don't decide for her. Neither do I."*

Vahlen received this. The exarch who has been watching Eden for forty days understood exactly what it cost to say that first.

### The Threat Model

Vahlen confirmed: liches are real. He encountered one approximately eighty years ago. Survived because it allowed him to survive — not the same as victory.

**Kill:** Find and destroy the phylactery. The only permanent solution.
**Contain:** Temporary. Requires power it cannot breach and vigilance that doesn't lapse. Patience eventually wins.
**Divert:** Give it something real it wants more than what it's pursuing. Requires forcing a choice, which patient things resist.
**Bargain:** Requires knowing the phylactery's location or the credible ability to find it. Leverage must be real.
**Avoid:** Not an option. It knows Eden's name. Has known it possibly since before Thornhaven.

### The Exarch Schism

Vahlen had suspected for approximately thirty years that the reformist/accelerationist divide was not organic. The slim volume confirmed what the suspicion couldn't prove. The schism was manufactured — pointed toward an outcome by something external. Control of Bhaal's succession. Insurance against the one thing that ends a lich.

Vahlen's admission, held quietly: *"I encountered one eighty years ago. I think now it let me survive because I was potentially useful. Someday. To something it hadn't decided yet."*

The possibility that Vahlen has been a piece, named and received without flinching: *"Then the most important thing I can do is be a piece that moves in ways it didn't anticipate."*

### The Personal Cost

Vahlen knew Sable. She asked him, approximately twelve years ago, whether he had ever encountered something that didn't die. He told her to be careful. She died anyway.

He has been carrying that for twelve years.

Eden gave him a word he hadn't realized he was missing.

*"Friend."*

Vahlen held it for a long moment.

*"I have not been called a friend. In a very long time."*

## TORMUND — SECOND VISIT

### The Threat Model Update

Tormund confirmed: the grimoires selected by Elara Moonshadow over eleven years represent a complete working library for the maintenance of lichdom. Not acquisition of new power. Maintenance of an existing state that is deteriorating.

The lich is not merely patient. It is frightened. Fifty years of patience becoming urgency because the trajectory of entropy is becoming undeniable. It needs those grimoires. The window is closing. Eden is between them and it.

### The Successor

The slim volume is a recruitment text. Written by the lich, not for it. The intended reader: someone already powerful, already knowledgeable, already close to the technical requirements — needing only the philosophical permission. The argument frames the ritual as an act of courage, not transgression, for someone who needs to believe they are being brave.

This means:
1. There are ten more recruitment vectors that haven't been found yet.
2. The destination needs to be made unattractive — the deterioration exposed.
3. The intended successor may already be in position. May not know what they are.
4. They are a victim until they aren't. To be found and helped, not arrested.

### The Weave Framework

The conversation with Tormund produced a four-part framework for understanding relationships with the Weave:

| Type | Example | Conscious | Willing |
|------|---------|-----------|---------|
| Wizard/academic magic | Eden, Tormund | Yes | Yes |
| Lyren's presence-based magic | Lyren | No | Yes |
| The disc | The disc | No | No |
| The lich | The lich | Yes | No |

The Weave is not passive. It responds to what is done within it. The lich has been wounding it — actively, repeatedly, for fifty years or more — and the Weave has been building a response. The disc is that response finding a focal point. Eden holding the disc gives the immune response hands and a mind.

The Portent may be the Weave communicating what it needs on a given day. Eden has been learning the language without knowing it. Day 23 may be the day he became fluent enough to hear it.

## THE KING

The rest of the case attached: the chain of recommendations, the grimoire selection, the deterioration inference, the successor theory, the slim volume, the companion text, Vahlen's testimony.

The King asked the right questions throughout. Received something enormous. Immediately asked: *"What do you need from me?"*

**Three things granted:**
1. Authority to accelerate grimoire security — whatever Tormund needs, no bureaucratic delays.
2. Authority to convene the divine council — formally, under Crown sanction.
3. Trust to move immediately when the phylactery location is found.

The King committed to being present at the council. Not as authority. As witness. To make Serath understand from the first moment that this is real.

On Sable: *"She'll be remembered correctly. I'll see to it personally."*

*"This ends."*

Not rhetoric. Just true.

### The Multiclass

Requires: Cleric 1, Cleric 2, Cleric 3. Three levels of showing up. Three mornings of bread tasting like bread. The Weave keeping score not of cleverness but of consistency.

## THE ARCHIVE — SECOND VISIT

### Reading Both Texts

Eden read both the slim volume and Sable's companion text in full. Annotated them. Synthesized the comparison into his journal.

**Where they agree:**
- The boundary between mortal and immortal existence is not absolute
- The phylactery is the key
- The ritual requires existing power and knowledge
- The process is deliberate, chosen

**Where they disagree:**
- The slim volume: death is political imposition, the ritual is courage
- The companion text: death makes choice meaningful, the ritual is fear — specifically fear of something

**What's missing from both:**
- Ritual details (deliberately omitted from the slim volume)
- The location of the wound in the Weave (Sable's text ends mid-sentence)
- What the lich feared specifically
- The nature of the phylactery in this specific case

**Sable's companion text ends:**
*"If the wound in the Weave can be located then the next step is to identify—"*

Nothing after that.

Eden added three words below the interruption, in smaller handwriting:

*—what it loved.*

### The Phylactery Insight

The companion text confirms: the phylactery is not chosen for power. It is chosen for significance. Personal relationship. Something that already knows its owner.

The lich severed every relationship except the one it couldn't.

*Find what it couldn't let go of. Find the phylactery.*

### The Target Realization

Eden is the person with the most intimate demonstrated knowledge of the Thornhaven grimoires. The lich has known his name possibly since before Thornhaven. The polygon — everyone Eden loves — is the leverage. Lyren most acutely.

*The lich will find my phylactery.*

Eden is Lyren's phylactery.

Named clearly. Not written down. Not in the Archive. Held.

## KETHRIS — THE BRIEFING

### The Schism

Kethris received the full picture. The lich. The manufactured schism. Forty-three years of choices made inside a frame someone else built.

Four hundred and seventeen kills. Six mass casualty events prevented. Lyren's eight months in a cell.

Her response, after a long silence:

*"I chose every one. Whatever the framework. Whatever was built around me. That's mine. The lich doesn't get that."*

### Dusk

Kethris confirmed two remaining accelerationist exarchs: Maren the Hollow and Dusk. The third is unclear.

Dusk's name was given by the accelerationists as a pejorative when Dusk withdrew. Not chosen. The name before is Dusk's to share, if trust is established.

Kethris and Dusk have history. Not hostile, not friendly. Two people who made different choices at the same crossroads, a long time ago. Now approaching that crossroads again from a different direction.

Personal. Kethris confirmed it. She wants to be in the room when Eden approaches Dusk. And doesn't want to be. Both things.

### The Council Strategy

**For Serath:** Tell him honestly. The space between. The splinter. The lich's presence in his territory for centuries. Vahlen carries the initial weight. Serath comes to Eden when ready, on his own terms. Eden receives whatever arrives.

**For Maren:** The lich is an abomination without theological exception. This isn't reformist or accelerationist. Serath's commitment carries weight because the cost of manifesting is visible — it demonstrates real stakes.

**For Dusk:** The pro-tempore framing. Serath did necessary work without recognition, without claiming, because it was right. Dusk will recognize that. From the inside.

**For everyone:** The council is the first thing in forty-three years not pointing exarchs at each other. The schism divided them. The council is the undoing of fifty years of careful work.

### The Note That Was Never Hers

Lyren has been feeling the lich's wound in the Weave her entire life. The note that's almost right but not quite. The frequency she couldn't resolve. She thought for years it was a flaw in how she listens.

It wasn't her. It was never her.

*"When the lich is gone, the note stops."*

Not a question. Just true.

### The String

Eden told her directly: she is leverage. The lich will come for what he loves. She's the most visible, most recent, most acute vulnerability.

Her response:

*"It gave up everything it loved. For the phylactery. Which means it has one string. And you have many. Which is harder to pull."*

The polygon as armor, not just protection.

### Many Strings

The known darkness — the hunger, the horror, what happened two nights ago — is already witnessed. By Lyren. By Kethris. By Aldrin. By Helm. By Vahlen now. The thing that can be weaponized as a secret has already been named in rooms that held it correctly. The exposure lands in a polygon that has already absorbed it.

Found something already known. Already survived. Already filed correctly.
