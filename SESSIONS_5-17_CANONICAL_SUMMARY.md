# Sessions 5–17: Canonical Campaign Summary
**Campaign**: The Thornhaven Shadow Crisis (Royal Court Arc)
**Player Character**: Eden Sagebough, High Elf Wizard (Level 3, Divination)
**Sessions Covered**: 5–17
**Preceding Summary**: See `SESSIONS_1-4_CANONICAL_SUMMARY.md`

---

## Arc Overview

Sessions 5–17 cover Eden's transition from field investigator to institutional operator. What begins as a formal recognition ceremony becomes, over the course of seventeen in-game days, a cascade of escalating crises: cult assassins, exarch civil war, a staged divine insurrection, a treason trial, and the emergence of a lich whose influence runs beneath centuries of Crown history. By Day 24, Eden has ended a divine civil war, arrested a Senior Councillor for his mother's murder, proposed and ratified a framework for divine accountability, and learned that every threat so far may be a symptom of something older and considerably more patient.

He is twenty years old. He is tired. He is not stopping.

## The Story: Sessions 5–17

### Session 5 — The Royal Court
*In-game: Day 4*

Eden, Aldrin, Rorick Stoneshield, and Miriam Crowsinger attended the Royal Court audience. Eden delivered comprehensive testimony on the Thornhaven Crisis before King Aldric III and the assembled court, then proposed two initiatives: a distributed-key demiplane security system for the recovered grimoires, and a filtered transcription using Comprehend Languages. Both were approved.

The session's pivot was Archmage Tormund. When Tormund publicly challenged Eden's proposal — correctly identifying it as an overreach from an untrained wizard who had bypassed the Academy entirely — Eden recognized his error and acted immediately. Through Magistrate Kellan, he arranged a private audience and offered to run reputational damage control: that evening at the recognition gala, he went systematically through the room assuring court figures that Tormund would be the indispensable magical expertise behind the project, with Eden merely providing the hands-on experience from having handled the grimoires directly. Tormund recognized that both the acknowledgment of error and the solution were genuine, not performed. The alliance formed that night and never required renegotiation.

**Outcomes**: Eden appointed Special Investigator to the Crown (500gp annual stipend, Royal Writ, palace quarters). Awarded the Star of Valor medallion (enchanted with protective wards). Immediate reward of 1,000gp. Rorick promoted to Commander of the Thornhaven region. Miriam granted the title Royal Consultant on Planar Matters with a 200gp annual stipend. Aldrin officially retired, financially secured by Cornelius Blackwater's endowment. Tormund loaned Eden his personal copy of *Principia Arcana* — an unprecedented gesture.

**Alliance network formed**: King Aldric III (royal favor), Archmage Tormund (mentorship and partnership), High Priestess Celeste (moral alignment, shared goal of destroying or permanently securing the grimoires), Magistrate Kellan (court politics mentor), Commander Brecht (professional respect), Cornelius Blackwater (business partnership, clear ethical boundaries maintained).

**Threats confirmed**: Three Bhaal cultists, survivors of the Thornhaven cell's ambush unit, are theologically mandated to assassinate Eden. Protection detail assigned. Palace quarters assigned partly for security.

**Player quote**: *"Wow, even I feel exhausted after all that. I hate politics."* Eden demonstrated exceptional political instincts and has never once stopped demonstrating them.

### Sessions 6–7 — The Cultist Operation
*In-game: Days 5–7*

Eden's first mornings as Special Investigator were spent building operational infrastructure. Fang detected a cultist conducting surveillance on the palace, which Eden recognized as the opportunity it was. Over Days 5–6, he designed and launched a multi-layered counter-operation: body doubles (fabricated by Tormund) deployed as decoys, a sleep deprivation campaign targeting the three cultists at a Dock Ward boarding house, and ongoing intelligence from Blackwater's network.

The grimoire transcription project began formally during this period. Tormund's Academy students are managing it. It has proceeded slowly and without incident, falling almost entirely off Eden's active attention — it re-emerges later only in connection with the lich threat.

**Cultist resolution**: One of the three "cultists" was Lyren Ashveil, Kethris Mournshadow's daughter, planted as a deep-cover operative eight months prior. The second was killed during the eventual breach of Room 9. The third was taken into Crown custody and cooperated for clemency.

**Elara Moonshadow**: The captured cult leader from Thornhaven — former Master Archivist, Bhaal-possessed for twenty years, and the moral fulcrum of Sessions 1–2 — disappears from the record at this point. Her fate is canonically unresolved. She was a victim of possession as much as a perpetrator of harm; whether the Crown showed mercy or justice was never established. This remains an open thread.

### Session 8 — The Warded Room
*In-game: Day 7, morning through preparation*

Eden discovered an invisible rat familiar inspecting his spellbook. Rather than eliminating the threat, he captured it and used it as a message relay: *"Talk to the people wearing the copper bracelets. They'll make it worth your while."* The familiar — Whisper, belonging to the reformist Bhaal exarch Kethris Mournshadow — stayed voluntarily.

Vane, a Rookery operative (Quill-level decision maker), arrived at breakfast and walked past Eden's protection detail without difficulty. She delivered Blackwater's overnight intelligence alongside a much larger revelation: the three cultists in Room 9 had lost operational control of their own ritual. An active threshold was opening to a pocket realm. The entity preparing to manifest was Vorthanis the Rendered, an accelerationist Bhaal exarch. Timeline to physical manifestation: Day 8–9. Potential casualties in the Dock Ward: 4,000 minimum.

Eden followed Vane's intelligence to a warded meeting room on the third floor of the Royal Archives — a room his father had been building and maintaining for thirty-seven years. The wards didn't merely block scrying; they detected and recorded attempts. Aldrin had thirty-seven years of surveillance data on everyone who had ever tried to eavesdrop on his private conversations.

**The full picture**: In the secured room, Eden convened Brecht, Miriam (revealed to have a long-standing Rookery arrangement), Vane, and Whisper. The situation: Vorthanis leads the accelerationist exarch faction (four of seven total Bhaal exarchs, all wanting resurrection through mass death). Kethris leads the reformist faction (three exarchs, opposing indiscriminate slaughter). Vorthanis had captured Kethris three days prior and was using her as a ritual anchor for the threshold. Inside Room 9, deep cover, was Lyren Ashveil — Kethris's daughter, who didn't know her mother needed rescuing.

**Aldrin's confession**: Aldrin confirmed what Eden had pieced together. He had been coordinating with Kethris for eleven years. The letter that brought Eden to Thornhaven was a deliberate arrangement — *"arranged opportunity, not arranged conclusion."* Miriam had been placed in Thornhaven eight months prior to wait for Eden. The entire twenty-year investigation was preparation. Aldrin had tried to build Eden's capabilities while preserving his agency in how they were used. *"Did I build you into something you didn't want to be?"* Eden's answer: *"I've come around."*

Critical additional detail from Aldrin's surveillance records: Vorthanis had been monitoring Aldrin for at least thirteen years. The most recent surveillance layer in the warded room was placed eight months ago — precisely when Aldrin was finalizing Thornhaven coordination. The second unknown signature (not Vorthanis, not the Rookery, not Crown) began two weeks ago, when Eden became operationally visible. This would later be identified as Vahlen the Warden, the third reformist exarch, evaluating Eden since his emergence — resolved when the ward message prompted the second Thornhaven trip in Session 14.

**Operational planning**: Eden was assigned frontline role — the most dangerous position, responsible for severing Vorthanis's binding on Kethris. Disguise Self scroll, smoke pellets, Tormund's loaned artifacts (Chime of Opening, Dimensional Anchor Ward, Bead of Force). Team: Eden (frontline), Miriam (support), Carr (extraction), Devin (anchor), Vane (tactical), Lyren (inside asset), Brecht (this-side anchor), Whisper (verification).

### Session 9 — The Pocket Realm Extraction
*In-game: Day 7, fourth bell through evening*

The extraction was executed with sixty seconds from breach to collapse. Eden entered disguised as Kethris. Nat 1 on initiative — dead last in a room with an accelerationist exarch. Subject One hit Eden immediately (5 necrotic, 12→7 HP). Miriam's Thunderwave eliminated Subject One. Lyren made her choice when she saw Whisper in Eden's hand: *"Where is she? Where's my mother?"* — and committed to the extraction team.

Eden sprinted through the open threshold, dropping smoke at the binding slab. Vorthanis's truesight required physical obscurement, not magical darkness — the smoke worked. Shield of Faith from Miriam. Eden reached Kethris, severed four of seven binding marks with the Chime of Opening (three destroyed instantly). Kethris: barely conscious, three days of torture evident. Bead of Force deployed to trap Vorthanis (dirty 20 on DEX save to avoid trapping himself). Dimensional Anchor Ward: blocked teleportation. Carr extracted Kethris. The realm collapsed with Vorthanis inside.

Vorthanis was killed — not banished. Malthor's suicide death burst caught him at low HP from earlier damage and finished him. He died to friendly fire from his own ally, screaming mid-sentence. His fifty-nine-year plan ended in exactly the kind of chaos he had spent decades trying to avoid.

**Outcomes**: Kethris extracted, critical condition, Temple of Helm. 4,000+ lives saved. Zero extraction team casualties. Operation classified — buried in Crown archives. Lyren given Crown protection and immunity (prior arrangement between Kethris and Brecht). Chime of Opening, Dimensional Anchor Ward, most smoke pellets lost in realm collapse.

Whisper formally introduced Eden to Kethris before extraction: *"Your father presented you as an option. Kethris evaluated you independently. She sent you because you think like her."* Kethris's first words to Eden, semiconscious: *"Thank you. For not leaving me there. For ignoring my orders to close the threshold. For being exactly as stubborn as your father."*

Brecht's orders: forty-eight hours off-duty. Write down everything you know about pocket realm extraction before you forget it. The Crown has zero institutional knowledge and Eden is now the only expert.

### Session 10 — Tormund's Lesson
*In-game: Day 8*

Eden leveled to 3 (Divination Wizard). Tormund's morning note: *"My study. Ninth bell. Bring your spellbook and the questions you're too tired to ask yet."*

Tormund's assessment of the extraction: *"That's not luck. That's not even just good tactics. That's fundamental understanding of how magic works at a level most third-year students don't reach."* When Tormund asked what Eden had been thinking when Vorthanis projected supernatural terror, Eden's answer — *"duty was stronger than fear, thought wasn't strictly speaking part of it"* — led to a critical teaching: those aren't emotional states, they're magical states. The Weave responds to certainty about what matters. This is foundational to understanding why Eden is a Divination wizard specifically.

**Portent introduced**: Two d20 results at dawn, usable to replace any roll. Tormund's discipline rubric for usage: someone will die if I don't use it, or the mission fails catastrophically. Those are the only two reasons. Eden internalized this and took it seriously — the Session 11 record of him spending an entire day with portent results of 18 and 20 and not using either is a direct demonstration.

**New spells learned**: Augury, Flaming Sphere. Three Augury practice scenarios with Tormund established the synthesis Eden would rely on: "arranged conclusions plus divination." You don't use Portent to guarantee optimal outcomes; you use preparation to make the likely outcome good enough that Portent is only needed to prevent catastrophe.

### Session 12 — The Insurrection
*In-game: Days 10–11*

Eden brought Aldrin to the Archives to help with cataloguing. Beneath a shelf in the sub-basement: a sealed stone chamber, lead-bound box, strong conjuration and necromancy signatures. Runes on the lid: *WARNING — CONTAINMENT. DO NOT OPEN WITHOUT AUTHORIZATION. SEALED BY ORDER: KING ALDRIC II — YEAR 1247.* The wards were keeping something in. They were at 67% integrity — a 27% discrepancy from projected natural decay. The inner layer: 43%, degrading three times faster than projected. Estimated time to failure: three to seven days. The conjuration signature was pulsing like a heartbeat and getting faster.

The contents: Vorthanis the Rendered's physical body. In 1246, newly elevated, vulnerable to other exarchs hunting new ascendants, he had voluntarily sought imprisonment for protection. Seventy-nine years of gathering power, untouchable. The contract contained a release clause: *upon insurrection*, he would resume his *original purpose as Death Herald* — facilitator of mass death events (plagues, famines, wars, disasters), preparing populations for Bhaal's harvest. Not a specific event. Any insurrection. He had spent fifty-nine years planning exactly what he would do with that power and optimal timing.

The impossible choice: destroy the body, release Vorthanis immediately as Death Herald. Leave it, release him in days anyway. Both options end with Death Herald. Kethris, consulted via secure channel: *"Sometimes the best move in an impossible situation is to change the game entirely."*

**Eden's plan**: Stage a fake coup against Kethris. Make it look like Eden (power-hungry, opportunistic) is threatening her life to seize control of the reformist faction. If the other exarchs respond to the challenge as real insurrection, it IS insurrection — the Weave interprets response, not intent. Contract releases early, before Vorthanis is prepared. All three accelerationists forced to manifest in chaos rather than executing a careful plan.

King's conditions for authorization: Temple confirms Kethris's consent and reformist positioning, civilian evacuation (structural assessment cover story, Archives and three-block radius cleared in twelve hours), Crown forces supporting Temple response, Eden accepts ongoing permanent Crown protection. Approved. Execution: tenth bell, Day 11.

**The performance**: Eden burst into the healing ward in the middle of Temple acolytes. *"WEAK. WORTHLESS. BHAAL NEVER LOVED YOU!"* Knife at Kethris's throat. *"DON'T EVEN THINK ABOUT IT, LYREN!"* Lyren's reaction was genuine — she didn't know it was theater. *"Eden, please—"* Crying, frozen. The air shifted. Something divine felt the challenge. Vorthanis, watching: *"INTERESTING."* Eden doubled down: *"OHHH, VORTHANIS. YOU, TOO, WERE WEAK. WHICH IS WHY I OBLITERATED YOU AND SCATTERED YOUR ESSENCE ACROSS THE LOWER PLANES."* Pure incandescent rage. Three rifts tore open simultaneously: Vorthanis, Malthor the Fleshrender, Vex the Silent. All three accelerationists, in the Temple, now.

Kethris stood up — she had been acting weak. *"Lyren, stand down. It's theater."* A fourth rift: reformist exarch, armored, warhammer. *"Two against three. I like those odds."*

**Combat**: Portent results for the day: 1 and 1. Both natural ones weaponized perfectly. Vex's first attack (natural 19+): replaced with critical miss. Vorthanis's Inflict Wounds (would hit): replaced with critical miss, spell backfired, 13 necrotic to Vorthanis. Eden went down twice (plague cloud, Vorthanis follow-up). Lyren used a healing potion to revive him the first time. Kethris used Cure Wounds the second. During Malthor's suicide death burst (43 necrotic, CON save DC 17), Lyren threw herself in front of Eden, blocking enough damage that he survived at -6 HP rather than instant death. Lyren went unconscious. Celeste paralyzed. Malthor's burst also hit Vorthanis — already low on HP, the damage killed him. *"NO! NOT LIKE THIS! NOT BY—"* Dead. All three accelerationist exarchs eliminated.

**Casualties**: Five confirmed dead — Brother Marcus (Temple paladin, executed by Vex while defending an unconscious ally), Private Corrin, Private Selene, Sergeant Daveth, Private Oren (Crown guards, Malthor's death burst). Brecht wounded but survived. Celeste paralyzed, survived. Eden died twice and survived both times. Lyren nearly died and survived.

**The King's assessment**: *"You taunted him into manifesting early, forced all three to respond to chaos instead of executing their plan, and then they killed each other? You're twenty years old. You just ended a divine civil war by making an exarch so angry he got himself killed."*

Aldrin, when Eden survived: *"Don't ever do that again."* Eden: *"I know."*

Day 12: Funerals for the five fallen. Temple of Helm, formal rites led by Celeste. Eden positioned himself at the edge of Crown officials, closer to the families than the other representatives, and spoke — gave grief a face, said their names in front of the court. The rest of Day 12 was Eden's catastrophic first attempt at having feelings in front of another person. He and Lyren spent time in the conservatory. Meditation attempt: failed (CON save failed, could not sit quietly for ten minutes). Competitive board games: she won Throne's Gambit, he suffered through the puzzle box. A scrying sensor — an enchanted raven — was discovered. Eden's instinct was to go to Lyren first, before command. He ran from the feelings that implied. Literally. Curled up. Lyren followed, sat with him on the floor. Came back with food. *"I just think you're nice and pretty and smart."* Lyren: *"I think you're also nice and smart and not bad looking."* Both immediately backpedaled. *"Just two friends having dinner."* *"Friends or whatever this is, we'll figure it out."*

Said 'Aldrin, you'll die alone surrounded by books if someone doesn't intervene.' Then she just kept showing up."* *"I thought she was interfering with my work. City outing, hand-in-hand. Operational protocols established for the relationship (capture, mind control, death, comms). The Captain/First Mate framework named: Captain is reason and operational composure, First Mate is physical desire — same ship, same destination, the First Mate does not navigate. Eden gave Lyren his copy of *Principia Arcana* and began teaching her divination tracking magic (operational partnership, mutual intelligence). She learned it in five days.

Eden accidentally mentioned marriage after two days of dating. He will never live it down.

Aldrin's first tea with Kethris took place during this period.

### Session 14 — The Thornhaven Mission
*In-game: Days 15–17*

Eden's first solo operation: travel to Thornhaven to meet the unknown watcher who had been identified (by prior Augury and intelligence) as almost certainly Vahlen the Warden, third reformist exarch. Lyren ran to the gates to say goodbye properly — not just a note. Eden gave her permission to read all of *Principia Arcana*, not just the tracking sections. *"I might not be afraid of a little enchantment."* She learned the Friends cantrip in five days.

Two ambushes en route. First, four cultists with a scrying-connected leader (killed, scrying focus cracked). A note found on the body: *"Investigator travels to Thornhaven. Ambush at old site. Kill or delay. Payment on proof. — V."* Payment in smuggler's gold, untraceable. The V did not belong to Vahlen — Vahlen later confirmed this explicitly.

Second ambush: a mercenary company hired by an unknown employer. Eden offered clemency for information. Captain Harrick took it: hired via intermediary, no direct contact with employer. Eden's nat 20 on initiative resulted in the guards never letting him live down the phrase *"it's unlikely"* followed by immediately trying to use Portent to un-jinx the word.

**The Vahlen meeting**: Former Royal Archivist (mortal life), died, became an exarch through accumulated knowledge of divine death and portfolio administration. Has been watching Eden for forty days. His opening: *"If I meant harm, you would already be dead. I am an exarch. You are a third-level diviner. The power differential is significant. But I do not want your death. I want your partnership."* What Vahlen offered: intelligence on threats where reformist interests and Crown safety align. What he wanted: a mortal operator with Crown legitimacy, tactical creativity unburdened by divine politics.

Eden's negotiation yielded a binding oath with real penalty (10% permanent divine essence forfeiture per violation), explicit protection of Kethris added at Eden's request, and performance protocols — which Vahlen found delightful. *"You want KPIs for being my mortal agent. That is the most Eden Sagebough thing I've heard all night."* The oath was verified by Brother Thaddeus. Vahlen's warning before departure: *"The cultist ambush signed 'V'? That wasn't me. Someone doesn't want us working together."*

Eden returned two days early. Lyren was waiting at the gates.

Was expecting a third party who didn't show. Lyren's read: the third party had a personal connection to Holt and felt abandoned, not merely stood up. V didn't show.

Day 19, night, healing ward: Aldrin and Kethris discovered reading aloud to each other from a botanical text on midnight violets. Vahlen manifested. He revealed that Holt had made contact with someone inside the reformist movement eight months prior — that person had been eliminated, just before the period of escalating reformist tension. He also introduced Serath: someone who died in Bhaal's service and didn't quite finish dying. Has been administering Bhaal's divine portfolio unofficially, in the space between, for an indeterminate period. Was waiting for someone to propose the right framework.

Eden proposed the middle path: Crown-sanctioned targeted purge of genuine evil. Necessary deaths only. Nobody innocent. Feeds Bhaal's coherence through purposeful rather than random death — satisfies the portfolio without mass casualties. The Divine Council framework: exarchs commit divine essence in escrow (final death stakes for bad faith), reports through theological and intelligence channels to the King, Helm of Helm as neutral drafter and notary (protection of the innocent is Helm's precise portfolio), independent target verification before Crown authority acts, Helm reports directly to the King if the council crosses any line. Vahlen: in. Serath: in. Kethris: implicitly in.

Key intelligence from Vahlen and Serath: V's endgame identified. Not theological — political. V wants to control whoever speaks for Bhaal. Power over a god. Has been using both accelerationist and reformist factions as tools for at least two years minimum. V is neither.

**Critical security note**: Thorne has been on Eden's protection detail consistently, has filed approximately forty pages of notes, has *requested* the assignment, and used the word "obviously" in Brecht's office. The word "obviously" is significant — it originated with Sable Sagebough and has since spread through the polygon by proximity. Thorne is inside the polygon's behavioral radius. Brecht may also be affected. This is filed and noted, not yet acted on.

**Key canonicals established**: Safe word: Wooden Raven. *"I love you"* — mutual, deliberate, said in the conservatory. Captain and First Mate framework active. Fang brokers intelligence between Eden and Lyren (bidirectional pipeline, asymmetric leak rates). Aldrin and Kethris: active file, dinner confirmed, contact ongoing.

### Session 16 — The Mechanism Has a Mechanism
*In-game: Days 19 (evening) through Day 22*

Before first bell, Day 20, Celeste came to Eden. *"Sable. In case your father never told you."* He hadn't. Eden's mother's name, spoken for the first time in his presence.

**Sable Sagebough**: Came to Helm's Temple at seventeen with questions about divine death and portfolio administration without a living divine host. Found Serath. Spent three years building the bones of what Eden would independently propose as the Divine Council framework — she had arrived at the same architecture twenty years earlier. Was killed by V (Aldric Voss) for it. Celeste lied to protect Aldrin and nine-year-old Eden, told them she died of illness. Regretted it for eleven years.

Serath, reached directly: *"She is not lost. But she is not free. Not yet. She is waiting."* Sable's resurrection: a seventh-level spell, requires level 13, diamond worth 1,000gp consumed. A long-term goal, not a present possibility.

**Aldric Voss identified and arrested**: Senior Councillor, thirty-two years, Infrastructure/Trade/Internal Security portfolio. V. Blackwater had known his identity for eleven years, pulled punches out of fear, sent an inadequate indirect warning to Sable before her death — not in time. He collected two memory crystals at considerable personal risk and voluntarily disclosed them, along with an eleven-year dossier, to Eden. Crystal one (eight years old): Voss ordering Sable's handling. Crystal two (fourteen months old): Voss and Holt discussing how to handle Eden by name, before his appointment. The King rendered private judgment before the Council hearing. Charges: treason, murder of Sable Sagebough, conspiracy to murder Eden Sagebough, subversion of internal security over thirty-two years, conspiracy to establish parallel sovereign authority. Verdict: guilty on all charges. Sentence: death. Eden arrived in the Council records room at eighth bell and cast Sleep before Voss could administer the poison he was reaching for. *"Handle it."* (Voss, eleven years prior.) *"Handled."* (Eden, eighth bell.)

Councillor Maren Holt arrested simultaneously by Thorne.

**Eden's speech to the Council**: Five movements — established personal proximity and professional neutrality, laid out the institutional pattern (Vassen, Morwick, Drost, Voss: *"the mechanism that should have caught it was the thing that was compromised, which means the mechanism itself needs a mechanism"*), presented the evidence, established that the conspiracy was against a solution Sable had already built (*"filed correctly, in plain view of all"*), and ended with the invitation: *"Gods help me, I will see finished what my mother started. Sable Sagebough, you did not die in vain."* 

The King had known Sable. She came to Court once, asked him whether a King could be trusted to judge a god. He had been thinking about it for twenty years. Councillor Ashfen (twenty-six years, oldest member) had known something felt wrong when she died and filed it away. *"Better late."*

**Origin of "obviously"**: Sable Sagebough, patient zero. She told Aldrin on their third meeting that he was going to marry her and he'd better get used to it. He said that was highly presumptuous. She said: *"Obviously."* The word has since propagated through the polygon by proximity — found now in Tormund, Vahlen, Thaddeus, the King. It arrives wherever Sable's work finds its hands.

**Polygon established as formal concept**: What began as Fang's joke — Eden observing three parallel relationships (Eden-Lyren, Aldrin-Kethris, Fang-Whisper) and asking whether to call it a love triangle or a love hexagon, Fang expanding it to everyone who cares about Eden — became operational vocabulary. The polygon is Eden's inner circle of trusted people, defined not by role but by the quality of their investment in his survival and judgment. As of Day 22 it includes: Fang, Aldrin, Lyren, Brecht, Thorne, Kethris, Vahlen, Celeste, Thaddeus, Tormund (adjacent), Whisper, Marta (palace kitchen), Blackwater (probationary), Ashfen (newly recruited), Daven (probationary), the King, and Sable Sagebough (founding member, posthumous).

Blackwater's status: probationary. Criminal negligence on file. One chance. Leaning toward redemption — Eden is a forgiving person — but nothing canonically explicit yet.

**The name under everything**: Blackwater's folder contains the name of the person who recommended Voss for his Council seat thirty-two years ago. Aldrin reviewed it Day 21 and confirmed it is a significant problem. The chain of recommendations extends back centuries. Working theory (strongly held, not yet confirmed): this is the lich's work. Voss was a piece, not the architect.

**Day 21, the conservatory**: During an extended conversation, Eden physically forced Lyren down without warning or consent. He caught himself within approximately three seconds and pulled back. Confessed fully to Lyren that night, to Kethris the following morning. Used the correct word in his own accounting. Kethris helped him work through the distinctions. The shame is correct size. The protocols are strengthened: Fang and Whisper always present, Lyren's explicit promise to tell someone in the polygon if the captain fails completely, Eden's promise to Kethris of the same. The horror kept present — not filed away, not quarantined. The mechanism.

A developing reflexive aversion to physical touch with Lyren was identified by Kethris as a different kind of First Mate coup — self-protective rather than other-directed, but still deciding for Lyren rather than with her. Named. Brought to Lyren before it became a wall. Not acted on.

**Sub-basement breadcrumb**: A slim volume, no title on spine. First line: *"On the matter of the persistence of will beyond mortal terminus."* Sable's handwriting. Dense theological philosophy — questions about what happens to intention when the body stops, whether will can outlast flesh. Filed accessible, not urgent. Connection to lich antagonist: point of rare consensus between reformist (undeath as affront to death cycles) and accelerationist (absorbed souls don't count toward Bhaal's resurrection) factions.

### Session 17 — What It Loved
*In-game: Days 22–23*

The lich confirmed. Deteriorating. Frightened. Actively recruiting a successor — someone already in position, possibly unknowing, a victim until they aren't.
