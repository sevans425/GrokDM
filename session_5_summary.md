     4	## Session Recap
     5	
     6	**The Royal Court & the Game of Thrones (That Eden Claims to Hate)**
     7	
     8	Session 5 was a masterclass in political navigation disguised as testimony about grimoires. Eden, freshly arrived at the capital with his father Aldrin and hedge witch Miriam, stood before King Aldric III and the assembled Royal Court to present the Thornhaven Crisis resolution and propose solutions for the recovered grimoires.
     9	
    10	### The Court Testimony
    11	
    12	Eden's presentation was comprehensive and brilliant: he recounted the entire Thornhaven investigation from the angry crowd at the inn through the shadow creature encounters, the multi-site ritual disruption, the grimoire recovery from Raven's Crag, the deception of Bhaal, and the journey to the capital. He emphasized collaboration with Rorick and Miriam at every step, painting a picture of diverse expertise coming together to solve an impossible problem.
    13	
    14	Then came the proposal: a distributed key security system where grimoires would be locked in a demiplane vault, accessible only through keys embedded in noble family heirlooms and distributed across every major noble house. Loss of a key would constitute high treason. The system prevents single-point failure—no more Elara Moonshadows walking out with apocalyptic texts.
    15	
    16	But Eden went further: he proposed filtered transcription using Comprehend Languages to copy plain text while leaving arcane notation locked away. Preserve the knowledge for scholars, destroy the weapon for cultists.
    17	
    18	The court was impressed. The King was intrigued. And Archmage Tormund was *furious*.
    19	
    20	### The Tormund Problem
    21	
    22	Tormund—the kingdom's foremost magical authority, head of the Royal Archives, thirty years of experience—had just been publicly corrected by a second-level wizard. His security system was implicitly criticized. His Archives' failure was highlighted. And he was assigned to a partnership he didn't want, using methodology he considered insufficient.
    23	
    24	The political tension was palpable. Eden had won the proposal but potentially made a powerful enemy.
    25	
    26	### The Private Alliance (or: How to Turn Your Enemy Into Your Mentor)
    27	
    28	During the court recess, Eden did something remarkable: he requested a private audience with Tormund. Not to gloat, not to consolidate political victory, but to *help*.
    29	
    30	In Tormund's private study, Eden laid out a simple offer: "Tell me who to talk to, what to say, to rebuild your reputation. I have the court's ear. Let me use it for your benefit. I don't care about court politics—I just need those grimoires secured, and I need your expertise to do it."
    31	
    32	It was honest. It was direct. It acknowledged Tormund's value while offering genuine partnership.
    33	
    34	Tormund, after careful assessment, accepted. He gave Eden a five-point action plan for reputation rehabilitation. And in an unprecedented gesture—one that shocked observers who knew Tormund's reputation—he *loaned Eden a book from his personal collection*. Principia Arcana, a first-year Academy text, to help close the gap between practical and theoretical knowledge.
    35	
    36	An alliance was born.
    37	
    38	### The Recognition Ceremony
    39	
    40	When court reconvened, King Aldric III formally recognized the Thornhaven heroes:
    41	
    42	**Eden Sagebough**: Appointed Special Investigator to the Crown with authority to investigate magical threats kingdom-wide. Awarded the Star of Valor (an enchanted medallion with protective wards). Given 1,000 gold pieces immediate reward plus 500 gold annual stipend.
    43	
    44	**Commander Rorick Stoneshield** (promoted from Captain): Commander of Thornhaven region with authority over all guard forces. 500 gold reward.
    45	
    46	**Miriam Crowsinger**: Royal Consultant on Planar Matters with standing invitation to advise the Crown. 200 gold annual stipend plus 300 gold reward.
    47	
    48	The King's words were significant: "You represent different paths to excellence. Together, you achieved what seemed impossible. Let that be a lesson to all assembled: the kingdom's strength lies in drawing upon the best of all traditions."
    49	
    50	### The Assassination Threat
    51	
    52	Before moving to celebration, the court addressed the ongoing threat: three cultists still at large from the attempted ambush. High Priestess Celeste delivered sobering news—these cultists weren't just dangerous, they were *theologically mandated* to assassinate Eden. He had embarrassed Bhaal six times. To restore their god's honor, they must kill the heretic who defied him.
    53	
    54	Commander Brecht assigned a protection detail immediately. Eden would stay in Royal Palace guest quarters under guard until the threat was neutralized.
    55	
    56	### The Reception: Alliance Building at Speed
    57	
    58	The evening reception in the East Gallery was where Eden implemented Tormund's rehabilitation plan with ruthless efficiency:
    59	
    60	**Item One**: Defer to Tormund publicly on methodology. ✓ Done with multiple nobles and court wizards, emphasizing Tormund's irreplaceable expertise.
    61	
    62	**Item Two**: Secure High Priestess Celeste's support. ✓ Eden met with Celeste privately, explained the alliance with Tormund, and secured her commitment to publicly affirm Tormund's essential role. Celeste went further—she shared Eden's desire to *burn* the grimoires, creating a powerful moral alliance. They agreed to formalize the oversight structure (Eden + Tormund + Temple representative + Captain Thorne) before work begins.
    63	
    64	**Item Three**: Father spreads collaborative narrative. ✓ Aldrin was already implementing this with Archive scholars, emphasizing complementary expertise rather than generational replacement.
    65	
    66	**Items Four & Five**: Emphasize Tormund's irreplaceability, deflect criticism. ✓ Eden spent the entire reception telling nobles, wizards, and merchants that he's "out of his depth" in theoretical matters and that Tormund's archmage-level capabilities are essential. He turned every compliment into praise for Tormund's expertise.
    67	
    68	**The Blackwater Negotiation**: Late in the evening, Cornelius Blackwater—wealthiest merchant in the kingdom—approached Eden with a business proposition. He recognized Eden's trajectory and wanted to invest early.
    69	
    70	Blackwater's offer: 500 gold annual retainer (doubling Eden's Crown income), wholesale access to spell components, private tutors for magical education, and funding for Aldrin's retirement. In exchange: advance notice of Eden's investigations so Blackwater's trading concerns could position resources for post-crisis reconstruction.
    71	
    72	Eden's response was direct and principled: "I'm all for a little insider trading, as long as I don't catch you burning towns down to get paid to rebuild them. If I get even a whiff that you're involved as a perpetrator, the deal is off. You become a suspect, not a partner."
    73	
    74	Blackwater respected the clear boundaries. Alliance formed, pending contract review.
    75	
    76	### The Exhaustion
    77	
    78	By the end of the reception, Eden was utterly drained. He'd been "on" for hours—managing impressions, building alliances, navigating politics he claimed to hate but demonstrated exceptional skill at. Miriam had left after an hour. Rorick was discussing military matters with Brecht. Aldrin was coaching Archive scholars.
    79	
    80	Eden made one final move: he used Message cantrip to report progress to Tormund (cheeky but effective). Tormund responded with dry approval: "Efficient. Presumptuous. But efficient."
    81	
    82	Then Eden took Blackwater's advice and made a graceful exit. His father accompanied him to their new quarters—comfortable rooms in the palace's east wing, third floor, with sitting area, fireplace, and real beds.
    83	
    84	Before sleep, Eden told his father: "It's all in service of getting those grimoires in a deep, dark hole. I plan tactically for people the same way I planned for shadow creatures. Once the transcription is done, I don't know where I'll go."
    85	
    86	Aldrin's response was gentle but important: "People aren't shadow creatures, Eden. They're more unpredictable. Don't lose sight of that."
    87	
    88	Eden collapsed into bed, exhausted but successful. In one day, he'd gone from traveling wizard to Crown agent with powerful allies, significant resources, and a target on his back.
